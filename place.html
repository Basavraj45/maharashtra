Raigad Fort is one of the most historically significant forts in Maharashtra and a symbol of Maratha pride . Located in the Sahyadri mountain range at an altitude of about 2,700 feet, it served as the capital of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj's empire. It was here that Shivaji Maharaj was formally crowned as the Chhatrapati in 1674, marking the birth of Hindavi Swarajya. The fort is known for its massive stone walls, strategic location, and breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys. Mahalaxmi Temple in Kolhapur, Maharashtra, is one of the most revered and ancient temples in India, dedicated to Goddess Mahalaxmi, also known as Ambabai. The temple is considered one of the Shakti Peethas, sacred spots where parts of Goddess Sati are believed to have fallen. It holds great spiritual importance for devotees of the Shakta (Goddess worship) tradition. The temple dates back to the 7th century and features stunning Hemadpanthi architecture with beautifully carved stone walls, intricate sculptures, and a peaceful atmosphere. The idol of Mahalaxmi is made of black stone and weighs around 40 kg. Devotees believe that visiting the temple and offering prayers brings prosperity, health, and happiness. Thousands of pilgrims visit during the Navratri festival, when the temple is decorated and special rituals are performed. The Mahadwar (main gate), Garbhagriha (sanctum), and Deepmalas (lamp towers) add to the grandeur of the temple complex. Kolhapur Mahalaxmi Temple is not just a religious site but also a symbol of the cultural and spiritual heritage of Maharashtra.
